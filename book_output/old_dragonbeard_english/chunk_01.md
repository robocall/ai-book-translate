Emperor Yang of the Sui, seeking stability, appointed Sima Yang as governor of Chang’an, a position that reflected the emperor’s increasing reliance on Sima’s influence amidst widespread political turmoil. Sima’s behavior was marked by arrogance and extravagance, deviating from traditional protocols for court officials. He habitually sat on his bed when receiving audiences, displaying lavish gifts and employing numerous servants, a practice considered inappropriate for a high-ranking official. This trend intensified as the reign neared its end.

During this period, Li Jing, a county magistrate dressed as a commoner, presented the emperor with a strategic proposal. Sima, despite his customary display of authority, was deeply impressed by Jing’s counsel and immediately adopted it. Later, a courtesan, known for her striking appearance, approached Li Jing with a red scarf, observing him intently. After Jing departed, the courtesan inquired with an official about his origins and residence. Following this, Jing secured lodging at a modest inn. That night, around midnight, a visitor knocked on his door – a man in purple robes and a hat, carrying a pouch. Li Jing questioned the visitor, who identified herself as a courtesan from the Yang family. She then revealed herself to be a beautiful young woman, dressed in elaborate silk, and bowed before him, much to Sima’s surprise.
