Li Jing and Liu Wenjing returned to Chang’an, and on a midday occasion, they visited Zhang at the Ma Xing Dong wine house. Accompanied by a donkey and a scrawny mule, Zhang and a scholar were already seated. Zhang, disguised as the Curly-Bearded Guest, engaged Li Jing in conversation, discussing corrupt officials and Li Jing’s character. Li Jing, surprised by this encounter, invited Zhang to sit and drink repeatedly. Zhang revealed a hidden sum of ten thousand coins, instructing Li Jing to bury it in a secluded location and meet him again at the Fen Yang Bridge. Upon their return, the scholar, the Curly-Bearded Guest, and Li Jing met with Liu Wenjing, who was playing chess. The Sui Emperor arrived, observing the game and exhibiting a noticeably restored demeanor. The Curly-Bearded Guest, upon seeing the Emperor, reacted with dismay, forfeiting the game and expressing a sense of hopelessness. He then requested to leave, advising Li Jing to focus on his mission and return to the capital. Zhang then arranged for Li Jing’s continued journey and a meeting with the Emperor the following day at a small residence.
