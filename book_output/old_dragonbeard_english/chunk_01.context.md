The Sui Emperor Yang Xuan appointed Sima Yang as governor of Chang’an, reflecting a shift in imperial favor and increasing instability. Sima’s lavish and unconventional behavior alienated traditional court norms. Li Jing, a county magistrate, presented a strategic proposal to the emperor, impressing Sima and leading to its immediate adoption. A courtesan, known for her beauty, engaged Li Jing in a debate, highlighting his strategic acumen. This interaction, coupled with Sima’s initial dismissive attitude, underscored the growing tensions between traditional authority and emerging talent. The episode culminated in the courtesan’s revealing herself to Li Jing, further emphasizing his reputation and challenging Sima’s established dominance.
