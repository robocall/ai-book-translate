The Sui Emperor’s instability continued to manifest through Sima Yang’s actions. Li Jing’s departure from Chang’an, coupled with his encounter with Zhang, heightened Sima’s suspicion. Zhang, posing as the Curly-Bearded Guest, engaged Li Jing in a series of unsettling exchanges, revealing his habit of consuming the hearts of those he deemed corrupt and offering cryptic observations about Li Jing’s character and potential. Li Jing, wary of Zhang’s motives and the Guest’s unsettling behavior, sought to understand the situation in Taiyuan, ultimately learning that the man was the governor’s son. This encounter further fueled Sima’s distrust of Li Jing and intensified the atmosphere of uncertainty surrounding the imperial court.  Li Jing, seeking to confirm the Emperor’s identity and assess the situation, traveled to Taiyuan with Liu Wenjing, arranging a meeting at the Fen Yang Bridge.  Zhang, disguised as the Curly-Bearded Guest, met them there, and upon the Emperor’s arrival, immediately recognized Liu Wenjing. The Emperor, traveling incognito and exhibiting a noticeably altered demeanor, confirmed his identity as the Sui Emperor.  Liu Wenjing, impressed by the Emperor’s apparent sincerity and authority, expressed his admiration. Zhang remained observant and cautious, assessing the situation. The encounter concluded with Zhang’s assessment that the Emperor’s situation was nearing a definitive resolution, and he requested that Li Jing also meet him.
