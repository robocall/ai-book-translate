The Sui Emperor’s instability continued to manifest through Sima Yang’s actions. Li Jing’s departure from Chang’an, coupled with his encounter with Zhang, heightened Sima’s suspicion. Zhang, posing as the Curly-Bearded Guest, engaged Li Jing in a series of unsettling exchanges, revealing his habit of consuming the hearts of those he deemed corrupt and offering cryptic observations about Li Jing’s character and potential. Li Jing, wary of Zhang’s motives and the Guest’s unsettling behavior, sought to understand the situation in Taiyuan, ultimately learning that the man was the governor’s son. This encounter further fueled Sima’s distrust of Li Jing and intensified the atmosphere of uncertainty surrounding the imperial court. Li Jing, seeking to confirm the Emperor’s identity and assess the situation, traveled to Taiyuan with Liu Wenjing, arranging a meeting at the Fen Yang Bridge. Zhang, disguised as the Curly-Bearded Guest, met them there, and upon the Emperor’s arrival, immediately recognized Liu Wenjing. The Emperor, traveling incognito and exhibiting a noticeably altered demeanor, confirmed his identity as the Sui Emperor. Liu Wenjing, impressed by the Emperor’s apparent sincerity and authority, expressed his admiration. Zhang remained observant and cautious, assessing the situation. The encounter concluded with Zhang’s assessment that the Emperor’s situation was nearing a definitive resolution, and he requested that Li Jing also meet him. Subsequently, Li Jing and Liu Wenjing returned to Chang’an, and on a midday occasion, they visited Zhang at the Ma Xing Dong wine house. Accompanied by a donkey and a scrawny mule, Zhang and a scholar were already seated. Zhang, disguised as the Curly-Bearded Guest, engaged Li Jing in conversation, discussing corrupt officials and Li Jing’s character. Li Jing, surprised by this encounter, invited Zhang to sit and drink repeatedly. Upon their return, the scholar, the Curly-Bearded Guest, and Li Jing met with Liu Wenjing, who was playing chess. The Sui Emperor arrived, observing the game and exhibiting a noticeably restored demeanor. The Curly-Bearded Guest, upon seeing the Emperor, reacted with dismay, forfeiting the game and expressing a sense of hopelessness. He then requested to leave, advising Li Jing to focus on his mission and return to the capital. Following this, Li Jing and Liu Wenjing returned to Chang’an, and shortly thereafter, news arrived of significant unrest in Southeast Asia, involving pirate activity and the establishment of a new kingdom. Recognizing this as the culmination of Zhang’s efforts, Li Jing returned to report the success to Zhang’s family, offering respectful greetings and celebratory toasts. He then understood the nature of Zhang’s achievement – not a heroic conquest, but a pragmatic response to a shifting political landscape, a correction of a misdirected ambition. Li Jing’s recognition of this shift underscored the importance of adapting to circumstances rather than pursuing predetermined goals. Or, as was noted, Zhang’s military methods owed a significant debt to the teachings of Wei Gong.
