Li Jing, having left Chang’an, encountered Zhang, a man with a distinctive red beard, at a roadside inn. Zhang offered Li Jing a meal of roasted mutton, procured from the market, and they ate it quickly, sharing a small knife. Observing Li Jing’s modest circumstances, Zhang explained that he possessed a certain ambition, revealing this only to those who genuinely inquired. He then revealed a disturbing custom: he routinely consumed the hearts of individuals he considered morally corrupt, a practice he now claimed to be ending.  Zhang identified Li Jing as a true man of substance, possessing the qualities needed to understand the situation in Taiyuan, and inquired about the man's identity and age. Li Jing described a man he had once seen, considered a genuine figure, and offered a surname – “same.”  Zhang stated the man was around twenty and served as a governor’s beloved son.
