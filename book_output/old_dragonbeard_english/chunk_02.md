Li Jing, having impressed Sima Yang with his strategic insights and successfully navigated a complex social situation involving a beautiful courtesan, found himself embroiled in a further, unexpected encounter.  Initially, Sima Yang expressed skepticism about the potential threat posed by Li Jing’s influence in the capital, dismissing the rumors surrounding the courtesan’s involvement. However, Li Jing’s subsequent decision to abruptly leave Chang’an, accompanied by a display of impulsive action and a ride on a donkey, raised Sima Yang’s suspicions.  This shift in Li Jing’s behavior coincided with the arrival of a strikingly unusual man – a tall figure with a prominent red beard – who appeared at the inn where Li Jing was staying.  This stranger, identified as Zhang, and his sister, the eldest, engaged in a peculiar and deliberately provocative display, attempting to calm Li Jing’s anger through a carefully orchestrated presentation of their appearance.  The situation escalated further when Zhang revealed his true identity – a figure known as the Curly-Bearded Guest – and his arrival seemed directly connected to the instability surrounding Sima Yang’s rule.
