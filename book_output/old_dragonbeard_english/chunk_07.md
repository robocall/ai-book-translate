Li Jing’s influence in Taiyuan, following his encounter with Zhang, brought a period of stability lasting approximately five years. Supported by the local Li clan, particularly his sister, who possessed strategic insight and influence, Li Jing’s efforts fostered prosperity and established him as a respected official. This success eventually led to recognition and advancement under the Sui Emperor, culminating in Li Jing’s appointment as a state official. Years later, news arrived of significant unrest in Southeast Asia, involving pirate activity and the establishment of a new kingdom. Recognizing this as the culmination of Zhang’s efforts, Li Jing returned to report the success to Zhang’s family, offering respectful greetings and celebratory toasts. He then understood the nature of Zhang’s achievement – not a heroic conquest, but a pragmatic response to a shifting political landscape, a correction of a misdirected ambition. Li Jing’s recognition of this shift underscored the importance of adapting to circumstances rather than pursuing predetermined goals.
