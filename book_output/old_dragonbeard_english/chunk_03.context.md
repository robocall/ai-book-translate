The Sui Emperor’s instability continued to manifest through Sima Yang’s actions. Li Jing’s departure from Chang’an, coupled with his encounter with Zhang, heightened Sima’s suspicion. Zhang, posing as the Curly-Bearded Guest, engaged Li Jing in a series of unsettling exchanges, revealing his habit of consuming the hearts of those he deemed corrupt and offering cryptic observations about Li Jing’s character and potential. Li Jing, wary of Zhang’s motives and the Guest’s unsettling behavior, sought to understand the situation in Taiyuan, ultimately learning that the man was the governor’s son. This encounter further fueled Sima’s distrust of Li Jing and intensified the atmosphere of uncertainty surrounding the imperial court.
