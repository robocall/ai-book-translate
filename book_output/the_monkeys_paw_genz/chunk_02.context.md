Mr. and Mrs. White, along with their son Herbert, reside at Laburnam Villa, a remote and isolated property. They are engaged in a chess game with Sergeant-Major Morris, a guest who has arrived unexpectedly. The evening is characterized by a cold, wet weather, the unsettling sounds of the wind, and Mr. White’s anxieties about the villa’s inconvenient location. Morris’s arrival introduces a further layer of strangeness and foreshadows the events to come. The atmosphere is tense, with subtle hints of impending doom. Sergeant-Major Morris, a veteran of the army, arrives unexpectedly at Laburnam Villa, seeking shelter from the inclement weather. He begins to recount tales of his travels and experiences, including a story about a monkey’s paw – a small, dried paw said to possess magical properties and the ability to grant three wishes. Mr. White, intrigued, asks about the paw’s origins, learning that it was created by an old fakir who intended to demonstrate the power of fate and the consequences of interfering with it. Herbert, ever the impulsive one, suggests that Morris use the paw to make wishes, further escalating the unsettling atmosphere. Morris attempts to dissuade them, but the family's curiosity is piqued, and the paw is brought into the room. The scene establishes a sense of foreboding and sets the stage for the tragic events to come.
