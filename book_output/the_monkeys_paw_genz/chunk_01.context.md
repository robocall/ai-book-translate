Mr. and Mrs. White, along with their son Herbert, reside at Laburnam Villa, a remote and isolated property. They are engaged in a chess game with Sergeant-Major Morris, a guest who has arrived unexpectedly. The evening is characterized by a cold, wet weather, the unsettling sounds of the wind, and Mr. White’s anxieties about the villa’s inconvenient location. Morris’s arrival introduces a further layer of strangeness and foreshadows the events to come. The atmosphere is tense, with subtle hints of impending doom.
