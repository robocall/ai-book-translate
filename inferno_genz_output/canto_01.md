In the middle of my messed-up life,
I was lost in this gloomy forest, totally off track.
It was so wild and messed up, like,
Seriously, trying to explain it would be a pain.
This forest was giving me major anxiety,
Like, remembering it just made me feel worse.
But I gotta tell you what happened, everything else is here.
I don’t even know how I got in there,
It was like, super foggy and I just lost my way.
But when I hit the base of the mountain,
Where the valley ended, it kinda calmed me down.
I saw this dude’s broad shoulders,
Already covered in sunlight – he’s the one who guides everyone.

That was a little relief from the panic,
That had been stuck in my head all night.
And as a person struggles to breathe when they’re exhausted,
Trying to get out of the sea,
They run towards danger, like, staring at it.
So, I was freaking out, trying to figure things out,
When suddenly this lion showed up, all angry and intense.
Then this she-wolf came after him, looking desperate.
It totally freaked me out, and I lost all hope.
Like, one person gets super stressed when they’re about to lose something,
And that’s exactly how I felt, stuck with this scary beast.

While I was backing away, I saw a ghost,
His voice was all muffled from not talking for ages.
When I saw him in that empty desert,
I yelled, “Help me!
Spirit or living person, what are you?”

He said, “I used to be a man, back when.
I was from Lombardia, and my family was from Mantua.
I lived in Rome when Augustus was around,
During the time of old gods and fake stories.
I was a poet, and I wrote about this guy,
Anchises, who came out of Troy.
But why are you back here, doing this?
Why aren’t you going up that nice mountain?”
“Are you Virgil? Like, the source of all this amazing writing?”
“Glory and light of all the poets!
I’ve been trying to read your book for ages, and I really respect you.
You’re the one who taught me how to write,
You’re the reason my writing is so good.
Look at that beast you escaped from!
Save me from her, please!”

“You’re freaking out because she’s making you feel it,” he said.
“She’s messing with your whole system.” He said, “You have to go a different way
If you want to get out of this crazy place.”
He said, “She won’t let anyone pass, and she’s way worse than death.
She’s always hungry, and she’ll keep wanting more,
Until this other dog shows up and destroys her.
He’ll do it with love and wisdom and virtue,
And he’ll make Italy safe again.
That’s why Camilla, a perfect girl,
And Nisus, Euryalus, and Turnus died for him.
He’ll keep chasing her until she goes to hell,
Where she was first released by envy.
I’m telling you this to help you,
So you can follow me and I’ll be your guide.
I’ll take you through this forever place,
Where you’ll hear people screaming and see lost souls,
Who are begging for death.
And the next people you’ll see are happy in fire,
Because they hope to be with the blessed someday.
If you want to go up there with them,
You’ll need someone better than me to lead you.
I’ll take care of you when I leave.” He said, “That King,
Who’s in charge of everything, is against him.
He judges me, and that’s why he’s sending me to his city.
He rules everywhere, and he has his throne.
It’s great to be chosen by him!” I said, “
Bard! By the God you didn’t worship,
I’m begging you to lead me where you said,
So I can see Saint Peter’s gate and all those people
Who are in such a bad situation.”

He kept moving, and I followed him closely.
